Rest is not a reward.

We opened the Hub with one belief: calm should be part of ordinary life, not a luxury you earn after everything else is done.

How it started

Serenity Hub began in 2023 as a Tuesday-night yoga class in a borrowed surf club hall. Twelve mats, a rented kettle, and a rule that nobody had to explain why they came.

The class kept filling, and the conversations after class kept running longer than the practice. It became clear people were not just looking for exercise. They were looking for a room that did not want anything from them.

Today the Hub is that room, seven days a week: a converted timber warehouse holding yoga and meditation classes, a maker studio for slow craft, a small shop, and a calendar of gatherings that mark the seasons.

What we hold to

one

Come as you are

No experience, no flexibility, no special clothing required. Every class is taught with options, and the door price never decides who belongs. Our Saturday community class is pay what you can.

two

Slow is a skill

Rushing is a habit, and so is its opposite. Everything we run, from breathwork to ceramics, is practice at doing one thing at a time, with full attention.

three

Together is easier

Calm is contagious. We keep classes small, we learn names, and we leave time after each session for tea. Most of our members met each other here.
